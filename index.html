What Happens to Your Prostate
If You Leave It Untreated

Unlike many health conditions, an enlarged prostate does not plateau. It follows a well-documented progression — and most men only realize how far it has gone when their only remaining option is surgery.

📈

The Prostate Continues to Grow Silently

BPH is a progressive condition. Without treatment, the prostate grows by an average of 1–2g per year. What is causing mild inconvenience today becomes a serious obstruction within 1–2 years.

😴

Sleep Deprivation Becomes Chronic

Waking up 3–5 times every night compounds over months into serious sleep deprivation — affecting your memory, blood pressure, immune function, mood, and your relationship with your spouse.

🫁

Bladder and Kidney Damage Begin

As the prostate blocks urine flow, the bladder strains harder to push urine through. Over time the bladder wall thickens and weakens. Chronic retention eventually backs up into the kidneys, causing permanent damage.

🚨

Acute Urinary Retention — A Medical Emergency

In advanced BPH, the urethra can become completely blocked. The man suddenly cannot urinate at all. This requires emergency catheterisation at the hospital — an extremely painful and frightening experience.

🔪

Surgery — With a Permanent Price

TURP and laser prostate surgery carry a well-documented risk of permanent erectile dysfunction and retrograde ejaculation. Many men who undergo prostate surgery lose their sexual function entirely. This is the outcome most men are trying to avoid — but inaction makes it inevitable.
